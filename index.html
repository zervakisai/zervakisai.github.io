~/research/flare.md   ·   Submitted · Hellenic Robotics Forum · Target: Drones (MDPI)

FLARE — Decision Optimization under Uncertainty

Submitted to Hellenic Robotics Forum  ·  targeting Drones (MDPI)  ·  first author

  • [01] Simulation framework with 450+ experimental runs — built from real OpenStreetMap data, statistically proving that optimization algorithms tuned for safety can paradoxically increase total cost when timing constraints exist.
  • [02] Configurable risk coefficient (ρ) — lets operators dial between conservative and aggressive decision strategies, making the trade-off explicit and auditable rather than hidden in algorithm defaults.
  • [03] Rigorous statistical methodology — Friedman tests, Wilcoxon signed-rank with Bonferroni correction, Cliff's delta effect sizes, and Shapley attribution for interpretable, reproducible results.
  • [04] Transferable finding — directly relevant to any system balancing risk vs. throughput: production scheduling under disruption, preventive-maintenance thresholds, quality-vs-throughput trade-offs, energy-vs-output optimization.
» The operational takeaway: "Safe" algorithms fail when timing is critical — directly relevant to any operation where downtime costs money. The same ρ-based framework is the method; the UAV is just the first tested instance.
